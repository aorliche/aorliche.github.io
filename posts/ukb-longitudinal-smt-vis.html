Change in connectivity after two years in the 2,722 subject UKB longitudinal cohort.

We have posted a preprint on the arXiv, techRxiv, and medRxiv servers about changes in resting state connectivity in the UKB longitudinal cohort. We find striking increase in the SMT-VIS connectivity compared to the rest of inter-network connections.

Prediction using specific inter-network connections as well as p-values.

We don't know the exact cause, but UKB-supplied ICA also predicts scan order around 80% of the time, giving us more confidence in the result.

Interestingly, cross-sectional cohort FC increases across the board with aging (except for the VIS-VIS connection), with large and highly significant changes.
